"The most valuable contribution a human expert makes to AI is not just labelling data — it's knowing which data matters, and why."

Five years of real customer interactions have given me something AI models need: an intuitive understanding of what customers actually mean — not just what they literally say. I identify primary and secondary intent, flag emotional tone, document the resolution logic that worked, and mark the edge cases where a standard response would fail. The patterns that repeat most in real interactions are where models need the most training. The cases that break the pattern are where they need calibration. I know how to recognise both — and document them in a way that makes models meaningfully better.
